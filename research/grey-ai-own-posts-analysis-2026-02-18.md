## Patterns the Winners Share

### 1. Carousels dominate engagement rate
The top 3 posts by ER are ALL carousels. LinkedIn counts every swipe as a click, massively boosting engagement rate. But beyond the metric inflation, carousels hold attention longer and create a "committed reader" effect.

**Recommendation:** Make carousels the default format for at least 50% of posts.

### 2. Bold unicode headlines stop the scroll
19 of 23 posts use bold unicode text (ð‹ð¢ð¤ðž ð­ð¡ð¢ð¬). The posts that DON'T use it ("Is AI safety for kids a priority for you?", "We've been listening", "Three schools") tend to get lower engagement rates despite sometimes high impressions.

**Recommendation:** Keep using bold unicode for the opening line of every post.

### 3. Binary frameworks create self-identification
The two highest-ER posts both use a "two types" structure:
- "Two professionals. Two opposite mistakes."
- "Type 1: codependent. Type 2: avoidant."

This forces readers to categorize themselves, which drives comments and shares.

**Recommendation:** Use more binary/dichotomy frameworks: Before/After, Type A vs Type B, "most people do X, the best do Y."

### 4. Provocative emotional hooks > informational hooks
- "Toxic relationship" (25% ER) vs "September is back-to-school season" (5.8% ER)
- "Prove your adaptability. Or risk being cut." (8.9% ER) vs "Homework time, not screen time" (4.7% ER)

Posts that create tension, fear, or curiosity in the first line dramatically outperform informational openers.

**Recommendation:** Lead with tension, not information. First 2 lines should create an emotional response.

### 5. Timely cultural tie-ins boost shareability
- Valentine's Day posts (Feb 2026) = highest engagement period ever
- Accenture layoffs (Oct 2025) = 2nd highest impressions
- ChatGPT feature drop (Sep 2025) = high ER despite low reach
- CES / back-to-school = moderate boosts

**Recommendation:** Plan content around cultural moments, AI news events, and seasonal hooks.

### 6. Question CTAs drive the only comments
Posts with comments (4 out of 23 posts total) ALL end with a direct question:
- "What's your AI relationship status?"
- "Which mistake do you see more often?"
- Week at a glance got a comment (had relatable content)
- "Set it, hear it" got a comment (practical parenting tip)

22 of 23 posts have 0 comments. Questions fix this.

**Recommendation:** End EVERY post with a specific, answerable question.

### 7. News-jacking punches above weight class
Posts referencing specific news (Accenture layoffs, ChatGPT features, OpenAI parental controls) get higher impressions because they ride trending search terms and hashtags.

**Recommendation:** React to AI news within 24 hours with a Grey AI take.
