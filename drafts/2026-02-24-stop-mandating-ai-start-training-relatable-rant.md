"Use AI or else" is not a strategy.

But that's exactly what some companies are telling their people right now.

We're seeing executives pressure senior managers to use AI in every task. No exceptions. No critical assessment. Just use it.

Here's what actually happens when you mandate AI adoption without training:

→ People paste sensitive data into chatbots without knowing the privacy settings
→ Teams use AI outputs without verifying them because they were told to move fast
→ Compliance risks pile up invisibly because nobody was taught what "high-risk AI use" even means
→ The people who get fired weren't the ones who refused AI — they were the ones the company planned to cut anyway

The irony is brutal.

Companies that pressure employees into AI adoption without training them first are creating the exact compliance and quality risks they think AI will solve.

Meanwhile, the companies actually winning with AI are doing something wildly different.

They're training people first.

Not mandating tools. Building understanding.

Not "use this or you're out." More like "here's what this can do, here's what it can't, and here's how to decide when to use it."

That's the difference between AI adoption and AI literacy.

One creates fear. The other creates confidence.

Your team doesn't need an AI mandate.

They need an AI education.

Which approach is your company taking?
