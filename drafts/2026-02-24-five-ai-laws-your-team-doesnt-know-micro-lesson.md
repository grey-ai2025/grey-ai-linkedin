27 AI-related laws have been enacted in the U.S. since 2023.

Most teams don't know about any of them.

Here are 5 deadlines coming that will affect how your organization uses AI:

1. May 2026 — the TAKE IT DOWN Act (federal)
Platforms must remove nonconsensual intimate imagery, including AI-generated deepfakes, within 48 hours of a valid notice. If your team generates images with AI, you need a policy for this.

2. June 2026 — the Colorado AI Act
Any "high-risk" AI system that makes consequential decisions about people (hiring, lending, insurance) requires risk assessments, disclosure, and governance frameworks. This is the broadest state AI law in the country.

3. July 2026 — Connecticut SB 1295
Automated decision-making amendments to their data privacy act. If AI is making decisions about consumers, new transparency requirements kick in.

4. January 2027 — New York RAISE Act
Frontier model developers must follow transparency, disclosure, and governance requirements. If you build on or fine-tune large models, this affects you.

5. January 2027 — California AB 853
Expanded AI transparency requirements, with additional provisions phasing in January 2028.

Meanwhile, the EU AI Act's "red line" prohibitions are already being enforced — banning social scoring, manipulative AI, and untargeted facial recognition.

The regulatory wave isn't coming. It's here.

The question isn't whether your team needs AI governance. It's whether you're building it before the deadlines or after the fines.

Which of these were you aware of?
