Everyone's deploying AI agents right now.

Almost nobody is asking who's accountable when one goes wrong.

Here's how the risk profile has shifted:

2023 - chatbots. The risk was a bad answer. You'd Google it and move on.

2024 - copilots. The risk was a bad suggestion. A human still clicked "accept."

2025 - agents. The risk changed shape. AI got access to your data, your tools, and the authority to act on your behalf.

2026 - autonomous agents. Multi-agent systems running in parallel, making decisions, accessing files, executing workflows — while you're in a meeting.

The capability scaled.

The oversight didn't.

The biggest bottleneck for AI agents right now isn't intelligence. It isn't cost.

It's security.

The more data and control you give an agent, the more it can help you AND the more it can hurt you.

And most teams are in full "yolo mode" — optimizing for usefulness over safety.

Here's what your team needs before deploying any AI agent:

→ What data can it access? (and what can't it?)
→ What actions can it take autonomously vs. what needs human approval?
→ Who is accountable when the agent makes a mistake?
→ How do you audit what it actually did?

If you can't answer all four, you're not ready.

AI agents aren't the risk. Deploying them without governance is.

What's your team's agent oversight look like right now?
