## Notes

"AI won't replace you. But someone who uses AI will."

That line has been posted on LinkedIn roughly 47 million times.

And in 2023, it was useful. It got people's attention.

In 2026, it needs an update.

Here's what the latest data actually says:

→ 94% of Americans want AI to AUGMENT their work, not replace them (Harvard Business School, 2026)
→ People support automating about 30% of job tasks — but fiercely resist automating care, creativity, and human judgment
→ Investors are predicting real labor displacement THIS year — not in some vague future

So which is it? Is AI replacing us or not?

Neither. The real story is more specific than a bumper sticker.

AI is replacing TASKS, not people. But it's replacing tasks faster than most people are learning new ones.

The threat isn't a robot sitting in your chair.

It's your role quietly shrinking because 40% of what you used to do is now automated — and nobody taught you what to do with the time you got back.

The people who thrive won't be the ones who "use AI."

They'll be the ones who know how to do the work AI can't — and can prove it.

Critical thinking when the data is ambiguous.
Judgment calls when the stakes are high.
Relationships that can't be prompted into existence.

That's not soft skill fluff. That's the new career moat.

So here's the updated line:

AI won't replace you. But if you don't know where AI ends and your value begins, you'll have a hard time explaining why you're still needed.

What skill do you have that AI will never replicate?
