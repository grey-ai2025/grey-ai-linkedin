AI isn't taking your job.

It's creating work that didn't exist six months ago.

Here's what the replacement narrative gets wrong.

We keep imagining AI as a 1:1 swap. One AI agent replaces one employee. The headcount drops. The org chart shrinks.

That's not what's happening.

We're seeing it across every industry we work with. The majority of AI agent workload isn't going to existing tasks. It's going to tasks that were never worth doing before because the headcount wasn't there.

Here's what that looks like in practice:

→ A real estate firm using agents to analyze every lease agreement across their entire portfolio for trends and opportunities — work they never had the headcount to do

→ A law firm deploying agents on contract types that were previously unprofitable to touch

→ A life sciences company running drug discovery analysis at a scale they couldn't afford with human researchers alone

→ Production studios using AI tools across half their pipeline — not eliminating creatives, but enabling them to work faster on more ambitious projects

The pattern is the same everywhere.

AI isn't shrinking what companies do. It's expanding what they can afford to attempt.

The companies that treat AI as a cost-cutting tool will save 20%.

The companies that treat AI as a capability multiplier will build things that didn't exist before.

One is optimization. The other is transformation.

Which one is your team building toward?
